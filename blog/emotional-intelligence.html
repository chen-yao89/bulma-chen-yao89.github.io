What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence, also known as emotional quotient (EQ), is the ability to understand and use your emotions in positive ways to help you to deal with any obstacles encountered in your life.

The four attributes:

  • Self-management

Control the impulse, maintain your emotions in healthy ways, take initiative, stick to commitments, and adapt to changing circumstances

  • Self-awareness

cognition of your own emotions and knowing the correlation between the emotions and your thoughts and behaviour. also know your strengths and weaknesses and have self-confidence

  • Social awareness

empathy, capable of reading the room

  • Relationship management

capable of developing and maintaining healthy relationships, communicating clearing, good influence and team player

How is it different to IQ?

IQ decides whether you are a fast learner, whether you can understand and apply high-level academic knowledge, or whether you can provide the best solution to a complex job at work. IQ is important because it decides the top ceiling of your academic achievement or your efficiency level at work.

EQ decides whether you can handle the stress well, whether you can control your own emotions, whether you can wipe out the negativity and conflict in any circumstances. EQ is the key to thriving in life.

Why is it important?

Most people in the world share an average level of IQ, which means the top ceilings of our potential achievements are the same or close enough. But why do failures and criminals exist? Why are some people living a happier life whilst others are surviving the miserable? It is EQ that differentiates the average us into many different groups. To put it in a simple term, the group with higher EQ thrives, the group with lower EQ survives or suffers.
